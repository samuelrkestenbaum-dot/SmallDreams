# Small Steps to Big Dreams - Launch Execution Pack

## Strategic Direction

Build a real children's edutainment IP made faster with AI.

Do not build "AI kids videos that make money." Build a repeatable story world parents can trust and kids can recognize.

The growth loop has two separate engines:

| Engine | Audience | Purpose | Main CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kids channel | Curious kids, parents, teachers | Publish polished 45-60 second animated stories | Watch the next story |
| Build-in-public channel | Creators, AI-curious parents, founders, educators | Show how the show is being built with taste and transparency | Follow the build |

## Brand Spine

Working title: Small Steps to Big Dreams

One-line promise:
Animated 60-second stories about inventors, artists, leaders, and dreamers, made for curious kids.

Tagline options:
- Big dreams start small.
- Tiny stories about people who changed the world.
- Little steps. Big ideas.

Tone:
Warm, curious, humble, storybook-clear. Never hustle-bro. Never "AI slop." The child should feel encouraged; the parent should feel safe.

Visual language:
Soft storybook animation, cozy color, expressive faces, clear physical objects, gentle movement, and a recurring final frame with the question: "What will you build?"

## Non-Negotiables

- Every episode needs doubt, mess, failure, or a mistake before the win.
- No income claims on the kids channel.
- No copying existing kids brands, mascots, clips, or channel formats directly.
- No "AI does everything" positioning.
- Scripts must be original, child-safe, and emotionally true.
- Historical figures should be simplified without pretending their lives were perfect.
- If an episode is aimed at children, set YouTube audience settings honestly during upload.

## Kids Episode Formula

Target length: 45-60 seconds.

| Moment | Time | Job |
|---|---:|---|
| Scroll-stop opening | 0-3s | Start with a vivid contrast or problem |
| Ordinary beginning | 3-12s | Show the person as a child or beginner |
| Trouble appears | 12-25s | Add doubt, mess, broken attempt, or mistake |
| Small step | 25-40s | Show the repeated action that teaches them |
| Breakthrough | 40-52s | Reveal the invention, idea, or contribution |
| Moral question | 52-60s | End with a kid-facing prompt |

Reusable ending:
"Every big dream begins with one small step. What will you build?"

## First 72 Hours

1. Publish the Henry Ford short on the kids channel.
2. Create a playlist called "Small Steps to Big Dreams."
3. Add the episode to the playlist.
4. Post build-in-public script #1: "I am building an AI kids show from scratch."
5. Post a creator-facing poll: Henry Ford is episode 1. Who should be episode 2: Amelia Earhart, Madam C.J. Walker, Katherine Johnson, or Walt Disney?
6. Pick the next 3 episodes and batch scripts before generating visuals.
7. Save a repeatable prompt format for every future episode.

## Weekly Production Workflow

| Step | Output | Owner Mindset |
|---|---|---|
| Research | 10 fact bullets, 3 kid-safe truths, 1 caveat | Do not over-mythologize |
| Script | 90-110 words | Simple, emotional, specific |
| Storyboard | 7-9 scenes | Every scene needs a clear object/action |
| Visual generation | 7-9 clips | Keep character style consistent |
| Voice | 3 narrator auditions | Choose warmth over drama |
| Music | Soft instrumental bed | Support, never distract |
| Edit | 45-60 second vertical MP4 | Pace for kids, not hustle content |
| Publish | Title, description, captions, playlist | Make it easy to understand |
| Review | Retention, rewatch, parent feedback | Improve the next episode |

## Metrics to Watch

Kids channel:
- First 3 seconds viewed
- Average percentage viewed
- Rewatches
- Playlist starts
- Subscriber conversion per view
- Parent/teacher feedback where comments are available

Build-in-public channel:
- Completion rate
- Comments with useful feedback
- Follows from creator/parent/educator audience
- Saves/shares
- Number of people asking for the workflow

## Decision Rule

For the next 10 episodes, optimize for repeatability and audience trust, not monetization. The monetizable asset is the IP, the production system, and the audience relationship.
