# Build-In-Public Short Scripts

These are creator-facing posts for TikTok, Reels, Shorts, LinkedIn video, or X video. They should live outside the kids channel.

## Script 1 - The Positioning

Visual hook:
Show the finished Henry Ford video playing on a phone or laptop.

Text overlay:
I am building an AI kids show, but not AI slop.

Voiceover:
"I am building a children's edutainment series with AI. But I am trying very hard not to make the thing everyone hates: generic, soulless AI kids videos. Episode one is about Henry Ford as a curious kid who took clocks apart, made messy machines, and kept learning. The goal is not to copy viral kids channels. The goal is to build a real story world, faster."

CTA:
"Follow along if you want to watch the whole thing get built."

## Script 2 - The Lesson From Market Research

Visual hook:
Show a blurred collage of "AI kids video" style hooks, then cut to your storyboards.

Text overlay:
The market research was useful. The blueprint was dangerous.

Voiceover:
"The more I studied AI kids video content, the clearer it got: the real money is often selling the dream that AI kids videos make money. I do not want to build that. I want to steal the useful part, which is the hook structure, and throw away the scammy part, which is the fake promise that AI does everything."

CTA:
"I am documenting the cleaner version here."

## Script 3 - The Narrator Test

Visual hook:
Rapid cuts of the narrator audition page, then the final Marlene version.

Text overlay:
The narrator changed the whole episode.

Voiceover:
"The visuals were working, but the voice changed everything. Some narrators sounded too adult. Some sounded too dramatic. Marlene had the warmest storybook feel, so I slowed her slightly to match the exact video length. Tiny production choices like that are what keep this from feeling disposable."

CTA:
"Next I am testing episode two."

## Script 4 - The Failure Problem

Visual hook:
Show clips where characters look cheerful, then cut to a messy garage/wobbly car scene.

Text overlay:
The first version was too cheerful.

Voiceover:
"One problem with AI video is that everyone can look weirdly enthusiastic all the time. But kids stories need struggle. Henry's machines had to wobble. People had to doubt him. The lesson only works if the mistake shows up before the win."

CTA:
"That is the rule I am using for every episode."

## Script 5 - The Two-Channel Strategy

Visual hook:
Split screen: kids episode on one side, production notes on the other.

Text overlay:
One channel is the show. One channel is the build.

Voiceover:
"I am separating this into two channels. The kids channel is just the polished story world. No AI hustle, no money claims, no behind-the-scenes. The build-in-public channel is where I show the tools, mistakes, prompts, voice tests, and strategy. Same project, totally different audiences."

CTA:
"That separation is the whole strategy."

## Script 6 - Episode Two Poll

Visual hook:
Four character cards: Amelia Earhart, Madam C.J. Walker, Katherine Johnson, Walt Disney.

Text overlay:
Who should episode two be?

Voiceover:
"Episode one is Henry Ford. For episode two, I am picking between Amelia Earhart, Madam C.J. Walker, Katherine Johnson, and Walt Disney. The format is always the same: a curious beginning, a real obstacle, a small step, and a kid-friendly lesson."

CTA:
"Comment the name you would want to see next."

## Script 7 - The Production Loop

Visual hook:
Show the pipeline as quick cuts: research, script, storyboard, clips, voice, music, edit.

Text overlay:
The repeatable system matters more than one video.

Voiceover:
"The real asset here is not one Henry Ford short. It is the repeatable system: research, script, storyboard, AI video clips, narrator auditions, music, edit, publish, learn. If I can make that system reliable, then this can become an actual series."

CTA:
"I will share each step as I build it."
